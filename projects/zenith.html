Design Guide

The Brand

Zenith is a clothing brand with an avant-garde urban style, which stands out for being environmentally friendly.

It is designed for those who care about dressing in an ecological and at the same time modern way. It specializes in various streetwear styles that facilitate personal expression without sacrificing responsible consumption.

Imagotype proposals

For this guide, the following three possible logo designs were created

1.

Based on the youthful city lifestyle, this logo conveys a sense of customization and great personality.

The hood, as a symbol of style and comfort, is outlined by arrows that recall the recycling symbol, conveying the ecological sense of the brand.

2.

This proposal has a modern, updated feel, with lots of movement.

The silhouette of an ankle boot is broken up into a leaf pattern, adding dimension and style.



3.

The edges of this design allude to the neon lights of the city, with rounded corners and continuous lines.

The silhouette of a turtle blends into that of a cap, drawing a parallel between urban fashion and current environmental awareness.

From the above options the second one was picked to represent the brand due to its distinctiveness and high impact, differentiating the brand from competitors.

Variants

Horizontal

The space between the pictogram and the brand name is 10px and they are aligned in the center.

Isotype

The diagonal of the anagram coincides with the Z, which is why a feeling of symmetry is sought.

Geometry and reductions

The brand isotype is made up of 4 units in width and height, in its horizontal variant the imagotype requires 14 units in total. Additionally, it is recommended to leave one unit around as a safety zone.

The smallest size recommended for the standard imagotype is 40px of width, which is equivalent to roughly a centimeter for printing.

Color scheme

The brand follows a monochrome colour scheme with black and white. This allows the imagotype to be translated into prints on different items of clothing, without limiting the design.

However, it is recommended to use opposite shades of colour, or shades with high contrast between the logo and the background.
